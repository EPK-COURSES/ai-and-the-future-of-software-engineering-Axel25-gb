Duolingo Announces AI-First Strategy

Published: September 4, 2025 (original announcement: April 28, 2025)  |  Publisher: Mind the Product
Duolingo AI-First Luis von Ahn Workforce Policy Content Generation

Background

Duolingo is the world's most popular language-learning platform, with hundreds of millions of users across 40+ languages. In April 2025, CEO and co-founder Luis von Ahn sent a company-wide email declaring Duolingo's transition to an AI-first company. The email was later shared publicly on LinkedIn and became one of the most widely discussed tech industry documents of 2025.

The AI-First Declaration

Von Ahn drew a direct comparison to the company's decision to bet on mobile in 2013:

"AI is already changing how work gets done. It's not a question of if or when. It's happening now. Betting on mobile in 2013 made all the difference. We are making a similar call now, and this time the platform shift is to AI. When there's a shift this big, the worst thing you can do is wait."

Key Policy Changes

  • Duolingo will gradually stop using contractors for work that AI can handle.
  • New headcount will only be approved if a team cannot automate more of their work.
  • AI use will be factored into hiring processes and performance reviews.
  • Departments must "reconsider their workflows" — the company frames this as "a fundamental cultural shift."
  • Employees will receive training, mentorship, and AI tooling within their functions.

The Results: Course Creation at Scale

148 new language courses created in one year with AI assistance.
This is more courses than in the previous 12 years combined.
7 popular languages made available in all 28 app languages.

The Framing: Augmentation, Not Replacement

Von Ahn was careful to frame the change in human terms:

"This isn't about replacing Duos with AI. It's about removing bottlenecks so that employees can focus on creative work and real problems, not repetitive tasks. AI isn't just a productivity boost. It helps us get closer to our mission."

He also emphasized that the change would require the company to "rethink much of how we work" and that "making minor tweaks to systems designed for humans won't get us there."

Criticism and Concerns

The announcement generated significant backlash, especially regarding the contractor workforce. Language experts, translators, and content creators — many of whom were contracted workers — noted that Duolingo's AI-first shift directly threatens their livelihoods. Critics argued the framing of "removing bottlenecks" obscures the real workforce impact on gig workers.

Relevance to This Assignment

The Duolingo case is significant because it demonstrates AI enabling something previously unfeasible at any cost: producing 148 courses in one year versus one course every few months. It is the clearest example in this research of AI not merely speeding up workflows, but fundamentally unlocking capabilities that did not previously exist at scale. It also raises the most direct questions about the human cost of AI transformation.
