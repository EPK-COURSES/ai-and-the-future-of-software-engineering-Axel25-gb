Shopify CEO Tells Employees to Prove AI Can't Do Jobs Before Asking for New Hires

Author: Beatrice Nolan  |  Published: April 8, 2025  |  Publisher: Fortune
Shopify Tobi Lütke AI-First Policy Hiring Workforce

Summary

Shopify CEO Tobi Lütke shared an internal memo with employees declaring that effective use of AI is now a "fundamental expectation" of everyone at Shopify. The memo, which Lütke posted publicly on X (formerly Twitter) after anticipating it would be leaked, marks a significant cultural shift at one of the world's leading commerce platforms.

Core Policy Changes Announced

  • Before requesting additional headcount or resources, teams must demonstrate why they cannot get the work done using AI.
  • AI competency will be added to performance reviews and peer questionnaires.
  • The expectations apply to all employees including the executive team and Lütke himself.
  • Employees are encouraged to experiment, share what they learn, and develop AI skills actively.

Key Quotes from the Memo

"Reflexive AI usage is now a baseline expectation at Shopify."
"Frankly, I don't think it's feasible to opt out of learning the skill of applying AI in your craft; you are welcome to try, but I want to be honest I cannot see this working out today, and definitely not tomorrow. Stagnation is almost certain, and stagnation is slow-motion failure. If you're not climbing, you're sliding."
"Learning is self-directed, but share what you learned."

Context and Industry Significance

Lütke is not alone in this approach. Klarna's CEO stated that the company's AI chatbot handles the work of 700 customer service agents, and has spoken openly about AI reducing headcount. Duolingo made similar announcements the same month.

The article notes concerns about AI hallucinations and the risks of relying too heavily on AI for high-stakes tasks. Representatives for Shopify did not comment.

The memo triggered widespread attention across the tech industry and prompted similar announcements from Box, Fiverr, and other companies.
