The Reality of AI-Assisted Software Engineering Productivity

What the data really shows about AI coding tools in 2025

Author: Addy Osmani (Engineering Leader at Google, author of "Learning Patterns")  |  Published: August 16, 2025  |  Publisher: Elevate (Substack)
Developer Survey Productivity Research GitHub Copilot 2025 Analysis

TL;DR

AI functions as a situational force multiplier — providing modest, uneven boosts that augment rather than transform engineering productivity. Studies show 20–30% productivity improvements, far from "10x" claims. Individual developers and those working on greenfield projects see speed boosts, but these gains are not yet translating to overall team or organizational productivity.

Adoption and Sentiment (Stack Overflow 2025 Survey, 49,000+ Developers)

84% of developers are using or planning to use AI tools — up from 76% the prior year.
60% view AI tools favorably — down from over 70% in 2023.
46% of developers do not trust the accuracy of AI output — up from 31% the year prior.
66% cite "almost right but not quite" AI solutions as their biggest time sink.
72% say "vibe coding" (letting AI generate entire programs) is not part of their professional work.

Key Studies Summarized

Study Setting Result
Microsoft Research / Peng et al. (2023) Controlled trial, freelance developers, HTTP server task 55.8% faster with Copilot
Microsoft + Accenture + Fortune 100 RCT (2024) 5,000 developers, real work settings 26% productivity increase; no quality drop
Google Internal RCT (2024) ~100 engineers, enterprise-grade task (~474 LOC) 21% faster (96 min vs 114 min)
METR Study — Becker et al. (July 2025) 16 experienced open-source developers, large codebases 19% slower with AI tools

The AI Productivity Paradox (Faros / DORA Data, 10,000 Developers)

  • Teams with high AI use completed 21% more tasks and merged 98% more pull requests.
  • But PR review times increased by 91%, creating a new human bottleneck.
  • Bug rates increased by 9% per developer.
  • Average PR size grew by 154% — making review harder.
  • No significant correlation between AI adoption and better organizational delivery outcomes at company scale.

Where AI Helps Most

  • ✅ Greenfield projects and prototyping
  • ✅ Boilerplate and repetitive code
  • ✅ Documentation and learning new technologies
  • ✅ Onboarding to large codebases
  • ✅ Debugging aids (feeding error logs to AI for analysis)

Where AI Struggles

  • ❌ Large, complex legacy codebases
  • ❌ Autonomous ("agentic") coding without human oversight
  • ❌ Tasks requiring deep creative insight or novel architecture
  • ❌ Code accepted too quickly without verification ("almost right" trap)

Key Conclusion

"The future of coding is likely human+AI, not AI-alone. Embrace the helper, but keep your hands on the wheel and your engineering fundamentals sharp."

AI coding tools are helpful assistants that, when used wisely, can make engineers moderately more productive and happier by automating some drudgery — but they are not a substitute for human insight, and they introduce new challenges (verification, coordination) that must be managed.
